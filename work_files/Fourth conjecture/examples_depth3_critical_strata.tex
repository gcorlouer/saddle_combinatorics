\section*{Depth-3 checks}

\paragraph{Goal.}
Verify the critical-locus stratification statement when \(L=3\).

\paragraph{Admissible labels.}
In depth three, the lifted cyclic rank-pattern labels are indexed by the pairs
\[
(0,0),(0,1),(0,2),(0,3),(1,1),(1,2),(1,3),(2,2),(2,3),(2,4),(3,3),(3,4),(3,5).
\]
For the rank-\(r\) critical locus, the admissible labels satisfy
\[
\rho_{34}=d_3-r,
\qquad
\rho_{03}=r.
\]

\paragraph{Consequence.}
The theorem says that each \(H_S\)-orbit in \(\mathcal C_{d,S}^r\) is uniquely
determined by the remaining rank data subject to these two scalar constraints
and the finite-difference inequalities defining
\(\widetilde{\mathcal R}_d^{\mathrm{wt}}\).

\paragraph{Full-rank case.}
If \(r=d_3\), then
\[
\rho_{34}=0,
\]
so only the chain rank patterns survive. This matches the known orbit
decomposition of the global-minimum fiber.

\paragraph{Status.}
Completed. The depth-3 specialization agrees with the general theorem and with
the full-rank benchmark.
