\section{Examples of stratifications}

We want to find examples of stratifications of the critical loci.

Consider a deep linear network of depth \(2\) learning the identity. Consider
the \(S\)-cyclic variety given by the system
\begin{align*}
W_1\Sigma_{XY}P_Q
&=
\begin{pmatrix}
    A^1_r & 0 \\
    0 & D^1_{s-r}
\end{pmatrix}
\begin{pmatrix}
    0 & 0 \\
    0 & I_{s-r}
\end{pmatrix}
= 0, \\
\Sigma_{XY}P_QW_2
&=
\begin{pmatrix}
    0 & 0 \\
    0 & I_{s-r}
\end{pmatrix}
\begin{pmatrix}
    A^2_r & 0 \\
    0 & D^2_{s-r}
\end{pmatrix}
= 0.
\end{align*}

For example, we have symmetries for all \(g \in \GL_d\) such that
\[
g_1W_1g_0^{-1}\, g_0\Sigma_{XY}P_Qg_2^{-1}
=
\begin{pmatrix}
    g_1A^1_rg_0^{-1} & 0 \\
    0 & D^1_{s-r}
\end{pmatrix}
\begin{pmatrix}
    0 & 0 \\
    0 & I_{s-r}
\end{pmatrix}
= 0.
\]

In the case of critical loci defined by the intersection of the \(S\)-cyclic
variety with the product map
\[
\mu(\theta)=
\begin{pmatrix}
    I_r & 0 \\
    0 & 0
\end{pmatrix},
\]
these symmetries still hold.

This will not be the case in general, for example when the elements of the
symmetry group do not leave the product map invariant. Although the product map
itself is equivariant under the symmetry group, it can still be stratified into
orbits.
